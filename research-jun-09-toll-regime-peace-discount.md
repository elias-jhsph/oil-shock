# The toll regime, the unsigned peace, and the $20 discount

**Research window: May 1 – June 9, 2026. Compiled June 9, 2026, ~23:00 ET.** Sources: two recovered deep-research corpora (run 1: 26 sources / 125 claims / 35 adversarial votes; run 2 salvage: 20 sources, incl. CNBC Jun 9, Axios May 28, IEA OMR detail, Capital.com May 19) plus 8 clustered verification agents (~150 additional web calls) run June 9-10. Confidence flags inline; disputes catalogued at the end.

**The headline:** Brent fell from $111.26 (Apr 28) to **$91.45 (Jun 9 settle)** — including the worst single month since COVID (May: −19%) — **while physical flows tracked the prior dispatch's *pessimistic* case**. Hormuz throughput was ~2.7 mb/d in April (IEA) vs. ~20 pre-war; Iranian seaborne exports hit a six-year low (~209 kb/d in May); China's total crude imports hit an eight-year low; the SPR drained 60 million barrels from the anchor date; US commercial crude fell six straight weeks; Cushing sits 2.4M bbl above its operational floor. The entire move is a **peace discount on an unsigned memorandum**: the US-Iran 60-day MOU "mostly agreed" May 28-29 still lacks Trump's signature, deadlocked on blockade sequencing. At 5:00 PM ET tonight — after the June 9 settlement — CENTCOM struck Iranian air defenses near Hormuz in response to a downed Apache. The market's peace bet will be stress-tested at tomorrow's open (June 10 electronic trade already +3%).

## Act I (May 1–15): Project Freedom, the toll regime, and the grind to $109

- **May 4 — Operation "Project Freedom" launched**: US Navy escort op to bring merchant ships *out* of the Gulf (~23,000 stranded sailors from 87 countries). Distinct from the port blockade.
- **May 5 — CMA CGM San Antonio struck** (Malta-flagged, Khalifa Port→Mundra) by a land-attack cruise missile in the strait; 8 Filipino crew injured. Attributed to Iran. (Prior draft dated this May 1 — corrected. An earlier French-flagged ship, CMA CGM Everglade, was hit April 18.)
- **May 5/6 — Trump paused Project Freedom** after <48 hours, citing "Great Progress" toward a "Complete and Final Agreement." The **port blockade continued throughout** — Adm. Brad Cooper (May 23): "zero trade into and out of Iranian ports."
- **May 5 — Iran established the Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA)**: vessels apply via email with ownership/insurance/crew/cargo disclosure; tolls reported at **$1–2M per transit, settled in yuan (and reportedly Bitcoin) to IRGC-linked accounts**. Operationally live May 18. India, Iraq, and Pakistan secured bilateral carve-outs. The US, EU, and Gulf states reject its legality. PGSA published a "controlled maritime zone" map from Kuh-e Mubarak to south of Fujairah.
- **Seizures and sinkings**: Iran seized the *Ocean Koi* (Chinese-owned, OFAC-sanctioned shadow-fleet LR1) May 8; the Indian cargo ship *Haji Ali* was struck and sank off Oman May 13; Iran seized the *Hui Chuan* (Honduras-flagged "research vessel," actually a Chinese-operated floating armory) May 14.
- **Prices ground higher on the deadlock** (uranium stockpile + tolls): Brent $101.27 (May 6) → **$109.26 May 15 peak** (+3.35% that day, as China declined to lean on Iran). WTI rose faster — and **settled above Brent on May 18–19** ($108.66 vs $107.48), a rare inversion as US refiners bid for prompt domestic barrels with Cushing draining.
- **May 19 — Senate advanced Kaine's war-powers resolution 50–47** (discharge motion, 8th attempt; Paul, Collins, Murkowski, Cassidy; aided by 3 GOP absences).

## Act II (May 18–29): the collapse — worst month since COVID

- **May 20**: biggest down day of the window — Brent −5.31% to $101.04, WTI −8.82% — on renewed deal optimism; the WTI premium unwound violently.
- **May 25**: Trump said he called off imminent strikes to let negotiations run (Brent −4.06% to $96.14). **May 26**: Iran vowed retaliation (+3.58%). **May 27**: ceasefire optimism, −5.31% to $94.29.
- **May 28 — both things at once**: Iran fired a Fateh-110 ballistic missile toward Kuwait (intercepted; debris hit Ali Al Salem Air Base, injuring US personnel, destroying an MQ-9) and sent 5 attack drones toward the strait — *and the same day* US and Iranian negotiators reached the tentative 60-day framework (Axios). CENTCOM called the missile an "egregious ceasefire violation."
- **May 29 — the 60-day MOU** (Pakistan-mediated, like the April truce): extend ceasefire 60 days; **unrestricted Hormuz transit (no tolls, no harassment); Iran demines (30-day clause reported but unverified); US blockade lifted "in proportion to restoration of commercial shipping"; no sanctions relief for strait reopening; nuclear talks within the 60 days** — Iran verbally agreed to long-term enrichment suspension and surrender of its **400 kg** of 60% HEU for reprocessing abroad (earlier drafts of this research said 440 kg — corrected). **Required Trump's sign-off — which never came in this window.** Rubio met Pakistan's deputy PM May 29 in Washington.
- **May 29 close: Brent $92.05, WTI $87.36.** May: Brent −~19%, WTI −16.5% — worst month since the 2020 COVID crash, with the strait still at ~5% of normal traffic.

## Act III (Jun 1–9): the unsigned deal and tonight's strikes

- **June 3 — the stalemate, precisely** (Bloomberg): US position is "relief for performance" — Iran opens the strait first (no tolls, mines out, no firing), *then* the blockade lifts, with **no sanctions relief for that step**; asset unfreezing (~$24B frozen; US floating redirection of some to Gulf reconstruction) comes only with nuclear performance. Iran's position: blockade lifts first. Trump **personally edited the draft** (enrichment + Hormuz language, per CBS); Vance: "hard to say when or if" he signs.
- **June 3 — House passed the war powers resolution 215–208** — first final passage in either chamber since the war began (GOP defectors: Fitzpatrick, Massie, Barrett, Davidson). Veto certain; no override math. Same day, an Iranian drone hit Kuwait's main airport (1 killed) after US strikes on Qeshm Island.
- **Blockade enforcement never stopped**: June 2 Hellfire disabling of a Kharg-bound tanker; June 5 VLCC *LENORE* seized; June 8 unladen tanker disabled in the Gulf of Oman. Cumulative by June 8: **134 redirected / 7 disabled / 42 humanitarian passed** (was 100/4/26 on May 23). The Wikipedia "blockade ended May 29" phrasing is wrong — verified against CENTCOM actions.
- **June 7–8 — worst Israel-Iran exchanges in months**, then a **Trump-brokered mutual halt June 8** ("this round of fighting is behind us" — Israeli officials; Iran reserves response if strikes in Lebanon continue). Not a formal ceasefire; separate from the US-Iran MOU track.
- **June 8** — US Army Apache downed off the Omani coast near the strait; both pilots recovered safely (drone rescue).
- **June 9 — settlement $91.45 Brent (−2.97%) / $88.20 WTI (−3.4%, seven-week low)** — catalyzed by the Israel-Iran halt plus **Energy Secretary Chris Wright telling CNBC that Hormuz ship traffic is "rising very meaningfully."** The mechanism behind Wright's claim: a **JPMorgan note dated June 4 estimates ~2 mb/d is exiting the Gulf on tankers with transponders switched off, with the US Navy quietly coordinating some exits** — the dark flow that also explains the Dated-premium normalization. **Then, 5:00 PM ET: CENTCOM precision strikes on Iranian air defenses, ground-control stations, and radar near Hormuz** (Qeshm/Bandar Abbas explosions reported); operation declared complete the same evening; IRGC vowed a "heavy response"; Iranian missiles/drones subsequently reported toward Bahrain and Kuwait. **June 10 electronic trade: crude +3% (Brent ~$94, WTI ~$91).**

## Why JPMorgan's May 9–30 "operational minimum" spike never fired

The window peaked at $109.26 (May 15) — elevated, but no non-linear event. JPMorgan's own June 5 verdict: *"Visible inventories are drawing somewhat more slowly than our initial projections and demand destruction is proving larger than expected."* Four buffers absorbed pessimistic-case flow losses:

1. **Demand destruction**: global demand fell **4.3 mb/d in April** (JPM — ~2x the peak 2008 loss). Seaborne crude imports, Feb→Apr: China −3.6 mb/d, Japan −1.9, Korea −1.0, India −0.76 (IEA). Both agencies now forecast outright contraction for 2026: **EIA −1.1 mb/d; IEA −420 kb/d to 104.0 mb/d** (−1.3 vs pre-war path; steepest −2.45 mb/d y/y in 2Q26, OECD −0.93 / non-OECD −1.5). IEA documents rationing/restriction measures in 13+ countries. China May imports: **7.8 mb/d, lowest since Oct 2017**.
2. **The stock flood**: 164 mb of the IEA's 400 mb collective action released by May 8 (90 gov + 74 industry-obligation relief), 210 mb more government stocks due through end-July. US SPR fell **409 → 349.2M bbl** (Apr 10 → Jun 5), including −35.6M in May alone (~1.27 mb/d average; peak week −9.9M). **Global observed inventories drew ~250 mb across March–April** (−129 / −117, ~4 mb/d); April on-land stocks −170 mb (−5.7 mb/d), OECD on-land −146 mb (−4.9 mb/d).
3. **Non-Middle-East supply at records**: "Producers outside of the Middle East pushed output higher and lifted exports to record levels" (IEA May OMR — note: this quote was previously misattributed to JPMorgan). **Atlantic Basin crude exports +3.5 mb/d since February**; US crude exports ~6 mb/d vs a 3.9 mb/d pre-war 12-month average. Americas 2026 supply growth revised up >600 kb/d to **+1.5 mb/d**. US weekly production hit **13.8 mb/d** (June 5 week) vs ~13.6 pre-war forecast; rigs rose 6 straight weeks: 544 (Apr 24) → **563 total / 431 oil** (Jun 5) — the shale non-response of March-April is ending at $90-100.
4. **The bypass build-out**: non-Hormuz routes carried **7.8 mb/d** in April (Saudi Red Sea ports, Habshan–Fujairah, Iraq via Türkiye/Syria trucking + Ceyhan ~230 kb/d, Iran's Jask). Gulf exports recovered to 10.5 mb/d in April (still −14 mb/d vs Feb).

**But the regional crunch was real**: Cushing fell six consecutive weeks to **22.4M bbl** (May 29) vs the ~20M operational floor — the proximate cause of the WTI>Brent inversions. And commercial crude fell **457.2 → 426.5M** (May 1 → Jun 5 weeks, −30.7M in 5 weeks, ~3% below the 5-yr average), with refineries at **95.3% utilization**.
