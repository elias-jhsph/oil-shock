# Ceasefire holds, oil reprices, blockade goes kinetic

**The April 7–8 ceasefire was extended indefinitely on April 21, but the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed and the US naval blockade went kinetic on April 19** — the first time a US warship has fired its deck gun on another vessel since Operation Praying Mantis in 1988, exactly 38 years to the day. Brent has round-tripped from ~$102 (Apr 13 close) down to ~$90 (Apr 17 trough) and back up to **$111.26 on Apr 28** — a fresh post-war high. The base/pessimistic case has firmed: Iran's last-minute peace proposal (Hormuz-for-blockade, nuclear deferred) was delivered via Pakistan on Apr 27 and rejected as "not good enough" by Trump on Apr 28. Wall Street has uniformly revised forecasts higher; Goldman raised its Q4 Brent target to $90 (from $80), Citi opened a path to $150–$180, and JPMorgan now warns of "operational minimum" inventory limits between May 9–30 that would produce non-linear price spikes. Macro data have been resilient: PPI undershot, retail sales beat, jobless claims are flat, and the S&P 500 hit an all-time high on Apr 27. But consumer sentiment registered its second consecutive record low (final April 49.8), Trump's approval has cratered to 33–37%, and only 34% approve of the Iran conflict.

This update synthesizes 60+ primary and secondary sources covering the April 13–28 window. Note key flags: (1) the Apr 13 baseline of "WTI ~$104, Brent ~$102" appears to refer to **intraday peaks** following the blockade announcement, not settles (CNBC settles were $99.08/$99.36); (2) the **SPR baseline of 243M bbl is almost certainly incorrect** — DOE/EIA confirm SPR is ~409–413M bbl, with the post-2022 low at 347M in August 2023; (3) the original "Goldman Brent $90 Q2" baseline was actually a Q4 number, and Goldman has since raised it.

## The ceasefire that wouldn't die — but barely lives

**The two-week ceasefire (set to expire ~April 21) was extended indefinitely by Trump via Truth Social on April 21**, citing the "seriously fractured" Iranian government and a request from Pakistani PM Sharif and Field Marshal Asim Munir. Trump simultaneously "directed the Military to continue the Blockade." The morning of Apr 21, Trump told CNBC's *Squawk Box* he did **not** want to extend; by afternoon he reversed. To PBS on Apr 20 he had warned: "Then lots of bombs start going off." US officials told Axios Iran was given **"three to five days"** to engage. By Apr 22, Trump claimed the blockade was costing Iran **"$500 Million Dollars a day…SOS!!!"** — a figure Kpler analysts (via NBC, Apr 27) put closer to **$200–250M/day**.

**No major US/Israeli airstrikes on Iranian territory occurred during April 13–28, but the blockade itself went kinetic.** Six confirmed enforcement events in 10 days: **April 19** — USS Spruance (DDG-111) fired its 5-inch MK 45 gun into the engine room of Iranian-flagged container ship M/V Touska in the North Arabian Sea after a 6-hour standoff; the 31st MEU rappelled from USS Tripoli helicopters and seized the vessel (sailing 17 knots toward Bandar Abbas, OFAC-sanctioned, owned by Mosakhar Darya Shipping). **April 21** — M/T Tifani (~1.9M bbls Iranian crude loaded at Kharg Island Apr 5) boarded "without incident" between Sri Lanka and Indonesia using USS Miguel Keith — **2,000 miles from the Persian Gulf**, signaling extension of enforcement to the Indo-Pacific AOR. **April 22** — Reuters reports interception of Deep Sea, Sevin, and Dorena in Asian waters. **April 23** — M/T Majestic X (Guyana-flagged VLCC, ~1.9M bbls) seized in the Indian Ocean en route to Zhoushan, China. Combined Tifani + Majestic X: ~3.8M barrels of Iranian crude diverted from China.

CENTCOM cumulative intercept count: **23 vessels by Apr 18, 25 by Apr 20, ~37 by Apr 25**. **No US casualties confirmed**; no Iranian deaths in the seizures (Touska crew evacuated before the gun fire). IMO Sec-Gen Dominguez: at least 10 seafarers killed cumulatively since the war began Feb 28.

**Iran retaliated at sea on April 22** — its first seizures of foreign vessels since the war began. IRGC Navy gunboats took the Greek-owned, Liberian-flagged container ship **Epaminondas** (94,769 dwt, Technomar Shipping; "extensive damage to bridge" from gunfire) and **MSC Francesca** (Panama-flagged, alleged Israel-linked) ~20 nm off Oman, moving them to Iranian waters near Qeshm. The IRGC also fired on but did not seize the UAE-owned **Euphoria**. Press Sec. Karoline Leavitt argued the seizures didn't violate the ceasefire because "these were not US or Israeli ships."

**Trump's Apr 23 escalation** was material: he ordered the Navy to **"shoot and kill any boat… putting mines in the waters of the Strait of Hormuz"** with "no hesitation," and tripled mine-clearing activity. Pentagon told the House Armed Services Committee on Apr 21 that mine clearing **could take 6 months** and is unlikely until the war ends. Axios (Apr 23) reported Iran laid additional mines that week — a **second mining round**.

## Diplomacy: from collapse to a Hormuz-for-blockade gambit

Pakistan mediation continued through the window despite the Apr 12 collapse. FM Araghchi visited Islamabad twice (Apr 25–26), bracketing a Muscat trip on Apr 26 (Omani-hosted talks attended by senior intelligence officials from "several countries"). On **April 27, Iran formally submitted a new proposal via Pakistan**: reopen the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for an end to the blockade and the war, **with nuclear talks postponed to a later phase**. Rubio (Apr 27 Fox) called the deal "unacceptable" but "better than past pitches"; Trump told the NYT he was "not satisfied"; on Apr 28 he posted: "Iran has just informed us that they are in a 'State of Collapse.' They want us to 'Open the Hormuz Strait'… SOS!!!" Trump cancelled the Witkoff/Kushner Pakistan trip on Apr 25 ("I see no point of sending them on an 18-hour flight").

**France's "peaceful multinational mission" advanced from announcement to operational planning.** On Apr 17, Macron and UK PM Starmer co-chaired an Élysée conference with **~50 non-belligerent states**, confirming establishment of an "independent and strictly defensive multinational mission" for merchant escort and mine clearance. On Apr 22–23, the UK hosted military planners from 30+ nations at Permanent Joint HQ Northwood; Starmer said "over a dozen countries have already offered to contribute assets." Germany's Chancellor Merz (Apr 27 Marsberg) called the entire US strategy "ill-considered" and offered minesweepers; Trump attacked Merz on Truth Social Apr 28. The mission is **still in planning, not operational**.

**Russia and China condemned but did not actively defy.** Xi (Apr 21) told Saudi Crown Prince MBS the strait "should remain open to normal navigation." China's Guo Jiakun called the blockade "irresponsible and dangerous." On Apr 24, OFAC sanctioned **Hengli Petrochemical (Dalian)** — one of Iran's largest "teapot refinery" customers (~400,000 bpd capacity) — plus ~40 shipping firms and 19 shadow-fleet vessels; Hengli shares fell 10% on Apr 27. Lavrov visited Beijing Apr 14–15 and offered Russian crude to backfill China; on Apr 27 Araghchi met Putin in St. Petersburg. **No confirmed kinetic Russian or Chinese naval confrontation with the US Navy occurred in the window.**

**Israel-Lebanon ceasefire (April 16, 17:00 EST)** — first direct Israel-Lebanon negotiations since 1983, a 10-day cessation extended to mid-May on Apr 23. Despite the ceasefire, near-daily Israeli strikes continued (6 Hezbollah killed in Bint Jbeil Apr 24; evacuation orders for 7 Lebanese towns Apr 26). Lebanese Health Ministry: **2,509 killed, 7,755 wounded** since fighting renewed Mar 2.

**Houthis remained quiet.** No new active attacks on Red Sea shipping during the window. UNSC Resolution 2812 (Apr) extended SG Red Sea reporting; passed 13–0–2 (China, Russia abstaining).

**Congressional war powers:** The Senate rejected war powers resolutions twice in the window — **Apr 15 (Kaine) failed 47–52** and **Apr 22 (Baldwin) failed 46–51** (5th vote of the year). Rand Paul was the only Republican voting yes both times; Fetterman the only Democrat voting no. The House version (Meeks) failed **213–214** on Apr 16 (Massie sole R yes; Golden sole D no). The 60-day War Powers Act mark falls April 29; the formal-notification deadline is May 1. Schumer pledged weekly votes "until Republicans see reason."

Per-voyage cost stack is **$6–10M above January baseline**. The Trump DFC backstop ($40B revolving reinsurance facility, in force from March) is partly absorbing this.

## Production and infrastructure: progress and setbacks

**Saudi Arabia: positive surprise.** On Apr 12, the Saudi Energy Ministry confirmed the **East-West Pipeline (Petroline) has full pumping capacity restored — the 700,000 bpd previously lost has been recovered.** This is a material upgrade vs. the Apr 13 baseline of 600K bpd capacity loss. Manifa offshore (300K bpd) restored. Khurais (300K bpd lost) repair timeline still not announced. Abqaiq throughput restoration not separately confirmed.

**Iraq: partial recovery, structural opportunity.** Basra Oil Company chief said pre-war 3.4 mbpd export levels could be restored within one week if Hormuz reopens. Production stabilized at ~900K bpd in the south. Following the Apr 7 ceasefire, Bloomberg/TankerTrackers confirmed **2M bbls of Iraqi oil and 4M bbls of Saudi oil exited Hormuz in a single day** before the blockade tightened. **Today (April 28), Iraq formally moved on the Basra–Haditha bypass pipeline**: PM Al-Sudani allocated **$1.5 billion for 2026** under the Iraq–China oil-for-infrastructure framework; total project ~$4.6–5B, capacity up to 2.25 mbpd, with routing to Aqaba (Jordan), Baniyas (Syria), and possibly Ceyhan. This is a **confirmed funded policy move**, no longer rumor.

**UAE Ruwais (922K bpd, ADNOC):** Gradual restart underway following the ceasefire (Egypt Oil & Gas reporting); full timeline unspecified. Habshan gas processing complex remains shut from Mar 19 missile-debris damage.

**Qatar Ras Laffan: 3–5 year repair confirmed.** Wood Mackenzie (via The National, Apr 9): unlikely to be fully online before end-August for partial sites; **Trains 4 & 6 (12.8 mt/y, ~17% of exports) require 3–5 year repair**; **replacement gas turbines have 2–4 year lead times — a structural bottleneck**. Force majeure on long-term contracts to China, South Korea, Italy, Belgium for up to 5 years. IEA quarterly: cumulative LNG supply loss **~120 bcm 2026–2030 (15% of expected global LNG supply)**.

**Kuwait: deteriorating.** **Apr 20 Bloomberg reported Kuwait declared a further force majeure** on crude/products exports, citing inability to meet full obligations even when Hormuz reopens. Mina Al-Ahmadi (346K bpd, drone-attacked Apr 3 and 5) full-capacity recovery 3–4 months. Bahrain Bapco Energies (400K bpd) hit Apr 5 and Apr 12 — force majeure remains. **Kuwait situation has worsened, not improved, since the Apr 13 baseline.**

**Iran domestic fuel:** Pre-war structural deficit (~750K bpd consumption vs. ~670K bpd refining); long queues at Tehran stations; government denies shortage publicly. Kharg Island struck Apr 7. **Bessent (Apr 24): Iran's Kharg Island oil storage is full, forcing shutdown of Iranian oil wells.** Kpler (Apr 27): Iran has 12–22 days of remaining oil storage.

**Valero Benicia (CA): closure confirmed**, with conflicting effective dates. Official end of refining ~April 30, 2026 (this week); operations effectively ceased late January/early February per energiesmedia.com. Combined with Phillips 66 Wilmington (closed Oct 17, 2025), **California has lost ~285K bpd of refining capacity**. UC Davis projects this adds ~$1.21/gal to CA prices by August, **independent of the war.**

**BP Whiting (440K bpd, Indiana):** Brief power outage week of Apr 26 caused unit shutdown — keeping Midwest prices elevated. **Phillips 66 Wood River (356K bpd, IL)** 45-day maintenance ongoing.
